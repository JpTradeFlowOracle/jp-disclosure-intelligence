Tender Offer (TOB) 14:00
Nice Corporation ナイス

Nice corrects tender offer disclosure, adjusting target's financial units from thousands to millions.

Nice Corporation issued a correction to its tender offer announcement for Yamadai Corporation, specifically regarding the target's consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ended March 2026. The initial disclosure mistakenly presented key financial figures in thousands of yen, which has now been corrected to millions of yen. This adjustment represents a thousand-fold increase in the reported scale of Yamadai's financials.

This unit correction is highly material for investors assessing Yamadai's financial performance and the implied valuation of the tender offer. The substantial change in magnitude fundamentally alters the perception of the target company's revenue and profitability.

  • Correction issued on June 2, 2026, for a tender offer disclosure originally dated June 1, 2026.
  • The correction pertains to Yamadai Corporation's consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ended March 2026.
  • Reported figures for revenue, operating profit, ordinary profit, and net profit attributable to owners of parent were corrected from thousands of yen to millions of yen.
  • For example, revenue was corrected from 4,102 thousand yen to 4,102 million yen.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the significant upward revision in the scale of Yamadai's financial performance, which impacts the valuation context of the tender offer.

LUCKLAND reorganizes executive roles and structure to enhance regional and headquarters coordination.

LUCKLAND is implementing a significant organizational restructuring and reassigning executive and fellow responsibilities, effective July 1, 2026. This move is designed to strengthen collaboration between headquarters and regional operations, aligning with the previously announced headquarters relocation planned by year-end 2026. The restructuring includes renaming the CS Support Division to Maintenance and establishing a new five-division framework.

This strategic realignment aims to streamline operations and improve efficiency by centralizing oversight of regional functions under new, broader headquarters divisions, potentially boosting overall corporate performance and strategic execution.

  • Changes to executive and fellow responsibilities are effective July 1, 2026.
  • The company's headquarters relocation is scheduled by December 31, 2026.
  • The CS Support Division will be renamed to the Maintenance Division.
  • A new five-division structure will be adopted: Sales, Construction, Design, Maintenance, and Corporate Management.
  • Mr. Satoshi Nakamori's sales leadership role is expanded from Kanto to encompass the entire Sales Division.
  • Mr. Kazunori Shida will lead the newly named Maintenance Division.

Earnings Forecast 15:30
DAISAN Corporation ダイサン

DAISAN's FY2026 operating profit significantly missed forecast due to increased costs.

DAISAN's fiscal year 2026 results showed a substantial miss on profit targets, primarily driven by higher operating expenses. While revenue largely met expectations, accelerated talent acquisition and increased labor-related costs, particularly in Singapore, weighed heavily on profitability. Additionally, higher SG&A expenses for strategic initiatives contributed to the shortfall.

This indicates short-term pressure on margins as the company invests in long-term capacity and strategic growth, potentially impacting near-term investor sentiment despite revenue stability.

  • FY2026 operating profit was ¥268 million, a 36.0% decrease from the ¥420 million forecast.
  • Net profit attributable to parent company was ¥262 million, falling 20.5% short of the ¥330 million forecast.
  • Revenue of ¥11,139 million was largely in line with the ¥11,500 million forecast, a minor 3.1% deviation.
  • Increased personnel costs from accelerated talent acquisition and higher labor expenses in Singapore were key drivers of the profit miss.
Investor Takeaway Investors should monitor whether these strategic investments in human capital and business expansion translate into future revenue growth and improved profitability to justify the current margin compression.
