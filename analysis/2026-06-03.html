Starsys Co., Ltd. スターシーズ

Starsys revises Q2 earnings forecast upward significantly due to strong battery and GPU server demand.

Starsys Co., Ltd. has significantly raised its second-quarter consolidated earnings forecast, driven by robust performance in its grid-scale battery storage and GPU server businesses. Revenue and all profit metrics are now expected to exceed initial projections, with the GPU server segment seeing substantial front-loaded orders. This revision reflects improved quarterly revenue and profit recognition, addressing prior period challenges.

This upward revision highlights strong demand in key high-growth technology segments, indicating successful strategic execution and improved operational efficiency in revenue and cash flow management. The company's decision to maintain its full-year forecast suggests a strategic balance between current performance and planned investments for future growth.

  • Revised Q2 revenue forecast increased by 43.3% to JPY 8.6 billion from JPY 6.0 billion.
  • Operating profit forecast for Q2 was raised by 12.0% to JPY 280 million.
  • Net profit attributable to owners of parent for Q2 is now projected at JPY 150 million, a 15.4% increase from the previous forecast.
  • The full-year earnings forecast for the fiscal year ending February 2027 remains unchanged, pending further review of H1 progress and growth investments.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the strong operational momentum in key tech segments, but also the unchanged full-year outlook which factors in future growth investments, and the reported decrease in revised EPS despite higher net profit, which implies significant share dilution.

Earnings Report 08:30
Tamura Corporation タムラ製

Tamura Corporation corrects fiscal year 2026 cash flow and individual balance sheet figures.

Tamura Corporation has revised its fiscal year 2026 consolidated financial results, primarily impacting the consolidated cash flow statement and individual balance sheet. The corrections resulted in a lower operating cash inflow and an improved net investing cash outflow, while the overall cash and cash equivalents at period-end remained unchanged. These adjustments reflect reclassifications and updated figures related to trade receivables, inventories, and proceeds from a business transfer.

While the total cash position is unaffected, the reclassification of cash flow components offers a more precise view of the company's operational efficiency and capital allocation, which is vital for evaluating its financial health and strategic direction.

  • Consolidated operating cash flow for the fiscal year ended March 2026 was revised downwards by 469 million yen.
  • Consolidated investing cash flow for the same period showed an improved net outflow, reduced by 468 million yen.
  • The revision introduced 495 million yen in proceeds from a business transfer within investing activities.
  • Individual total assets for the fiscal year ended March 2026 were adjusted upwards by 69 million yen.
Investor Takeaway Investors should review the revised breakdown of cash flows, noting the lower operating cash generation and the new proceeds from a business transfer, to gain a clearer understanding of the company's underlying financial performance and strategic shifts.

Company corrects minor equity reference figure in prior fiscal year earnings report.

Double Standard has issued an amendment to its consolidated financial results for the fiscal year ended March 31, 2026. The correction addresses a minor misstatement in a reference equity figure, which was previously reported incorrectly. This amendment does not alter any core financial performance or position metrics.

The correction is clerical in nature, specifically adjusting a reference equity value without impacting the reported consolidated total assets, net assets, equity ratio, or net assets per share. This indicates no material financial restatement or operational change.

  • The original reference equity for March 31, 2026, was 6,712 million JPY.
  • The corrected reference equity for March 31, 2026, is 6,679 million JPY.
  • No changes were made to total assets, net assets, equity ratio, or net assets per share for the period.
  • The correction has no impact on the income statement or overall balance sheet figures.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note this is a minor clerical correction with no material impact on the company's reported financial performance or position.

The company reported its latest share repurchase activity, acquiring an additional 224,900 shares for approximately 230 million yen in May. This brings the cumulative buyback to nearly 47% of the maximum shares targeted under the current program, demonstrating consistent capital return efforts. The program is on track to enhance shareholder value.

This ongoing buyback program signals management's commitment to improving capital efficiency and potentially supporting the stock price through reduced share count. Consistent execution reinforces investor confidence in the company's capital allocation strategy.

  • In May 2026, 224,900 common shares were repurchased for 229,860,600 yen.
  • The current buyback program, approved in March 2026, targets up to 2,000,000 shares or 3,000,000,000 yen by November 30, 2026.
