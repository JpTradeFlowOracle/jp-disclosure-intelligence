Earnings Report 11:00
ニチアス

Nichias forecasts record profit for FY2027/3, driven by semiconductor recovery and strategic investments.

The company reported mixed results for FY2026/3, with revenue slightly below forecast but net profit significantly exceeding expectations due to tax benefits. While high-performance products faced headwinds, automotive parts and building materials showed profit improvement. Looking ahead to FY2027/3, the company projects record-high performance, anticipating a strong rebound in the semiconductor market and continued strategic investments.

This outlook signals confidence in a cyclical recovery for key segments and the effectiveness of ongoing profitability improvements. The substantial capital allocation plan, including strategic investments in R&D and M&A, alongside robust shareholder returns, underscores a proactive approach to long-term growth and value creation.

  • FY2026/3 net profit of ¥316 billion significantly beat forecast by 22.6%, aided by tax reductions.
  • FY2027/3 revenue is projected at ¥2,700 billion and operating profit at ¥450 billion, both record highs.
  • The company plans a 1-for-3 stock split and a FY2027/3 annual dividend of ¥65 per share (post-split), plus up to ¥5 billion in share buybacks.
  • Strategic investments include a partnership in fusion energy development and a delayed core system launch to May 2027.
  • The equity ratio improved to 77.7%, and the credit rating was upgraded to A+ (Stable).
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the strong profit outlook for the coming year, supported by a semiconductor market recovery and strategic capital allocation, alongside a commitment to enhanced shareholder returns and a robust balance sheet.

Share Buyback 11:00
東ソワール

Tokyo Soir completes restricted stock compensation issuance to executives.

Tokyo Soir has finalized the issuance of 17,400 treasury shares as restricted stock compensation to its internal directors and executive officers. This action, following a board resolution, aims to align management incentives with long-term shareholder value and enhance corporate governance.

This move strengthens executive retention and motivation, fostering a performance-driven culture. It signals the company's commitment to modern compensation practices and improved management accountability.

  • 17,400 common shares were issued from treasury stock.
  • The shares were issued at JPY 1,078 per share, totaling JPY 18,757,200.
  • Recipients include nine internal directors and executive officers.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note this as a positive step towards aligning management's interests with long-term shareholder returns and robust governance.
