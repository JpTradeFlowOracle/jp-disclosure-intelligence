Market Briefing

Today's corporate disclosures revealed a blend of strategic capital allocation and portfolio adjustments across Japanese markets. Companies focused on enhancing shareholder value through share repurchases and aligning executive incentives with performance. Concurrently, there was significant activity in strategic acquisitions and substantial capital raises targeting high-growth technology sectors. The asset management industry also demonstrated a clear trend of rationalizing underperforming funds while money market funds continued to exhibit stable growth.

The financial sector exhibited a dual trend, with asset managers actively streamlining their product offerings by liquidating sub-scale index funds, while money market funds continued to attract stable inflows. In the technology sector, companies are aggressively pursuing growth through strategic acquisitions and substantial capital raises to fund expansion in areas like AI and space infrastructure. Space Exploration Technologies finalized a massive global offering, securing over $72 billion for its AI and space ventures, signaling significant expansion. Data Applications made a strategic acquisition of an AI/DX firm, bolstering its tech capabilities. Meanwhile, Daiwa Asset Management announced the liquidation of multiple index funds due to persistently low assets, reflecting a broader trend of optimizing fund portfolios.

Extraordinary Report 09:23
Seven & i Holdings Co., Ltd. 株式会社セブン＆アイ・ホールディングス

Board approves new performance-linked stock compensation plan for executives.

Seven & i Holdings' board has approved a new post-delivery stock compensation plan, including Performance Share Units (PSUs) and Restricted Stock Units (RSUs), for its executives and outside directors. This initiative aims to align executive incentives with long-term shareholder value and company performance, utilizing treasury stock for allocation.

This move enhances corporate governance by linking executive remuneration directly to performance metrics and continued service, while the use of treasury stock avoids immediate share dilution from new issuance.

  • Board resolved on June 11, 2026, to grant PSUs and RSUs.
  • Up to 1,356,570 PSUs and 180,083 RSUs will be granted to eligible directors.
  • The compensation is valued at approximately JPY 2,981 million, based on a JPY 1,941 reference share price.
  • Shares will be allocated from existing treasury stock.
  • PSU performance metrics include consolidated EBITDA, ROIC, and relative TSR.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note the company's commitment to performance-based executive incentives and the use of treasury stock, which minimizes immediate dilution while potentially impacting future share buyback capacity.

  • Fund established: December 2013

Extraordinary Report 09:58
Daiwa Asset Management Co. Ltd. 大和アセットマネジメント株式会社

Daiwa Asset Management to redeem D-I's Foreign Stock Index fund due to persistently low assets.

Daiwa Asset Management has decided to initiate procedures for the early redemption of its D-I's Foreign Stock Index fund. This decision stems from the fund's consistently low net asset value and beneficiary units since its establishment in December 2013, making its continued operation unviable.

This move reflects the asset manager's rationalization of its product lineup, discontinuing funds that have failed to attract sufficient assets. It underscores the competitive pressure on fund managers to maintain viable asset under management (AUM) levels for their offerings.

  • The D-I's Foreign Stock Index fund is scheduled for early redemption on September 25, 2026, pending a written resolution.
  • As of April 2026, the fund's net asset value stood at JPY 140 million.
  • The fund's beneficiary units totaled 30 million as of April 2026.
  • The fund was initially established in December 2013.
Investor Takeaway Investors should note that asset managers actively manage their fund portfolios, and funds with persistently low AUM are subject to early redemption, impacting long-term investment strategies.
